## Executive Summary

**Overall:** Dunning Ship Rate improved from 65.59% to 66.79% (+1.20pp) in W16 as the cluster transitioned from Pre-Payday to Payday phase, with volume increasing by 11.2% to 30,340 eligible orders.

**Funnel Analysis:**

| Step | Check | Δ Conv | Result |
| ---- | ----- | ------ | ------ |
| Pre-Dunning AR | Stable (93.84% → 93.77%) | -0.1% | ✅ |
| Discount % | Slight increase (13.81% → 13.91%) | +0.7% | ✅ |
| PC2 | Strong improvement (40.35% → 46.22%) | +14.5% | ✅ |
| Ship Rate | Improved (65.59% → 66.79%) | +1.8% | ✅ |

**Key Findings:**
- **Payday effect driving gains:** The Pre-Payday → Payday transition correlated with strong PC2 improvement (+14.5%) across the cluster, lifting overall Ship Rate
- **AU and NO led performance:** AU improved Ship Rate by +7.0pp with lower discounts (-8.7%) and strong PC2 gains (+23.3%); NO showed the highest PC2 surge (+32.3%) alongside a +7.4pp Ship Rate increase
- **NZ is a notable outlier:** Despite Payday phase and strong PC2 gains (+29.3%), NZ Ship Rate declined -13.2pp, accompanied by a significant discount increase (+17.8%)
- **GB volume surge:** GB volume increased 22.8% WoW, the largest absolute volume growth, while Ship Rate improved +4.3pp despite slightly higher discounts
- **Mix shift consideration:** NO volume increased 59.5% WoW with high Ship Rate (88.24%), positively contributing to cluster-level improvement

**Action:** **Monitor** – Overall performance is healthy with expected Payday uplift. **Investigate NZ** specifically to understand why Ship Rate declined despite favorable PC2 conditions; the discount increase (+17.8%) may indicate pricing or product mix issues unique to this market.
